System Overview

Pneumatic System Components

1. Pressure Cylinder: Stores compressed gas (300-3000 PSI)
2. Regulator Valve: Controls output pressure to firing chamber
3. Firing Valve: Releases gas burst to propel pellet
4. Barrel & Chamber: Guides pellet trajectory
5. Seals & O-rings: Prevents gas leakage
Safe Pressure Range:
0-3000 PSI

Common Issues

  • Pressure loss - leaking seals/O-rings
  • Weak firing - valve malfunction
  • No firing - blocked valve or empty cylinder
  • Inconsistent power - regulator failure
  • Gas leak - damaged threads or seals

Always depressurize completely before any repair work!

Safe Disassembly Procedure

1 Depressurize System
  • Close cylinder valve completely
  • Fire gun repeatedly until no gas releases
  • Verify zero pressure with gauge
  • Wait 5 minutes for pressure equalization
2 Remove Cylinder
  • Unscrew cylinder counter-clockwise
  • Inspect cylinder threads for damage
  • Check cylinder O-ring condition
3 Access Valve Assembly
  • Remove grip panels/screws
  • Locate valve housing bolts (usually 2-4)
  • Carefully remove valve assembly
  • Note spring orientation and order
4 Disassemble Firing Valve
  • Remove valve retaining pin/clip
  • Extract valve stem carefully
  • Remove O-rings with pick tool
  • Inspect all sealing surfaces

Photo Tip: Take photos at each step to remember assembly order

Repair Procedures

Seal & O-ring Replacement

1 Inspect O-rings
  • Look for cracks, flattening, or hardening
  • Check for proper size and thickness
  • Replace if any damage visible
2 Install New O-rings
  • Clean grooves thoroughly
  • Apply thin layer of silicone grease
  • Stretch O-ring gently into groove
  • Ensure O-ring seats properly (no twisting)

Valve Repair

3 Clean Valve Components
  • Use isopropyl alcohol or degreaser
  • Remove all dirt and old lubricant
  • Check valve seat for scratches/pitting
  • Polish minor imperfections with fine sandpaper (1000+ grit)
4 Replace Springs if Weak
  • Compare spring length to new spring
  • Test spring tension by compression
  • Install replacement if worn/compressed

Thread Seal Repair

5 Reseal Threaded Connections
  • Clean threads with wire brush
  • Wrap Teflon tape clockwise (3-5 wraps)
  • Apply thread sealant for high-pressure fittings
  • Hand-tighten, then wrench 1-1.5 turns

Over-tightening can crack brass fittings! Tighten until snug, not maximum force.

Reassembly Procedure

1 Lubricate Moving Parts
  • Apply silicone grease to O-rings
  • Light coat on valve stem
  • Avoid excess - can attract dirt
2 Reassemble Valve
  • Insert valve stem with O-rings
  • Install spring in correct orientation
  • Secure retaining pin/clip
  • Check valve moves freely
3 Install Valve Assembly
  • Align valve housing carefully
  • Tighten bolts in cross pattern
  • Torque evenly - don't over-tighten
  • Reinstall grip panels
4 Attach Cylinder
  • Check cylinder O-ring lubrication
  • Thread on clockwise - hand tight
  • Use wrench for final 1/4 turn only
5 Pressure Test
  • Fill to low pressure first (500 PSI)
  • Check for leaks with soapy water
  • Listen for hissing sounds
  • If no leaks, increase to normal pressure
  • Let sit 10 minutes and recheck

Leak Detection: Spray soapy water on connections. Bubbles indicate gas leak.

Troubleshooting Guide

Problem: Gun Won't Fire

  • Check: Cylinder pressure - refill if empty
  • Check: Valve blockage - clean valve passage
  • Check: Trigger mechanism - ensure proper engagement
  • Check: Safety engaged - disengage safety

Problem: Weak Power/Velocity

  • Check: Pressure too low - increase regulator setting
  • Check: Worn valve seal - replace O-rings
  • Check: Barrel obstruction - clean barrel
  • Check: Weak valve spring - replace spring

Problem: Continuous Gas Leak

  • Check: Damaged O-rings - replace all seals
  • Check: Valve not seating - clean valve face
  • Check: Cross-threaded fitting - reseal threads
  • Check: Cracked cylinder - replace cylinder

Problem: Inconsistent Shots

  • Check: Regulator malfunction - rebuild regulator
  • Check: Partial blockage - flush system
  • Check: Temperature variation - allow pressure equalization
  • Check: Contaminated gas - use clean air source

When to Seek Professional Help

  • Cracked pressure vessel/cylinder
  • Damaged barrel rifling
  • Stripped threads on critical parts
  • Persistent leaks after multiple repairs
  • Any safety mechanism failure

Maintenance Schedule

  • After each use: Wipe down exterior, check for leaks
  • Every 500 shots: Clean barrel, inspect seals
  • Every 6 months: Full disassembly, replace O-rings
  • Annually: Complete valve rebuild, pressure test

Quick Reference: Pressure Settings

  • Target Shooting: 800-1200 PSI
  • Hunting Small Game: 1500-2000 PSI
  • Maximum Safe: 3000 PSI (check gun rating)
  • Testing/Break-in: 500-800 PSI

  • Valve stem: 010 (10mm x 1.5mm)

Why oxygen is deadly as propellant:

  • Oxygen supports combustion - any spark = explosion
  • Oils and greases ignite spontaneously with O‚ÇÇ
  • Metal parts can combust in pure oxygen
  • Use ONLY compressed air or nitrogen

Personal Protective Equipment

  • Safety glasses: ALWAYS wear when working
  • Gloves: Protect from sharp edges and lubricants
  • Ear protection: For high-pressure testing
  • Ventilation: Work in well-ventilated area

Storage Safety

  • Store gun unloaded and depressurized
  • Keep cylinders in cool, dry location (below 50¬∞C)
  • Away from heat sources, flames, direct sunlight
  • Store upright, secured to prevent falling
  • Keep out of reach of children
  • Lock in safe or use trigger lock

Pressure Safety Rules

  • Never exceed: Gun's maximum rated PSI
  • Depressurize before: Any maintenance work
  • Pressure test: Point gun in safe direction
  • Leak check: Use soapy water, never open flame
  • Gradual filling: Fill slowly to prevent shock

Emergency Procedures

If Gas Leak Occurs:

  • Immediately close cylinder valve
  • Move to well-ventilated area
  • Point gun in safe direction
  • Release remaining pressure by firing
  • Do not attempt repair until fully depressurized

If Cylinder Damaged:

  • Do NOT attempt to refill damaged cylinder
  • Slowly release pressure outdoors
  • Dispose according to local regulations
  • Never weld, drill, or modify pressure vessels

Lubrication Technique:
  • Use ONLY silicone-based grease (petroleum breaks down rubber)
  • Apply thin, even coat - excess attracts dirt
  • Reapply every 3-6 months or 1000 shots
  • Store spare O-rings in cool, dark place

Improving Accuracy

  • Barrel cleaning: Clean every 200-300 shots
  • Consistent pressure: Use regulator for uniform power
  • Pellet quality: Use consistent, quality pellets
  • Barrel crown: Never damage muzzle edge
  • Break-in period: First 100-200 shots settles seals

Cost-Saving Tips

  • Buy O-rings in bulk: Assortment kits save money
  • Prevent damage: Regular maintenance cheaper than repairs

üéØ Performance Tuning Guide

‚ö†Ô∏è Tuning Safety Warning

Never exceed manufacturer's maximum pressure rating. Over-tuning can cause catastrophic failure, injury, or death. Test in safe area with protective equipment.

Barrel Tuning

1 Barrel Cleaning Method
  • Use cleaning rod with cotton patches
  • Apply bore cleaner or gun oil
  • Push patches through (don't pull back)
  • Repeat until patches come out clean
  • Final dry patch to remove excess oil
2 Barrel Polish (Advanced)
  • Use JB Bore Paste or similar
  • Apply thin layer on felt pellet
  • Push through barrel 10-20 times
  • Clean thoroughly after polishing
  • Test fire - should see velocity increase

Expected Results: Polished barrel can increase velocity 20-50 FPS and improve consistency

Valve Tuning

Spring Modifications:
  • Lighter spring: Less force, faster cycling, lower power
  • Heavier spring: More force, higher power, slower cycling
  • Preload adjustment: Add washers to increase tension
  • Custom cut: Remove coils to reduce length (test carefully)
Valve Port Enlargement (Expert Only):
  • Increases air flow to chamber
  • Can boost power 10-25%
  • Requires drilling/reaming skills
  • Irreversible modification
  • Risk of ruining valve body

‚ö†Ô∏è Only attempt if experienced with machining!

Regulator Tuning

1 Adjustable Regulator Setup
  • Start at lowest pressure setting
  • Fire 10 shots, measure velocity
  • Increase pressure by 100 PSI
  • Repeat until desired velocity reached
  • Fine-tune in 25 PSI increments

Sweet Spot Finding: Look for pressure where velocity is highest with minimal shot-to-shot variation (standard deviation under 15 FPS)

Pellet Selection for Performance

Light Pellets (7-10 grains):
  • Higher velocity (900-1100+ FPS)
  • Flatter trajectory
  • Less accuracy at long range
  • Better for close-range target shooting
Medium Pellets (12-16 grains):
  • Balanced velocity (750-900 FPS)
  • Best all-around performance
  • Good accuracy and energy
  • Recommended for most uses
Heavy Pellets (18-25+ grains):
  • Lower velocity (600-750 FPS)
  • Maximum energy transfer
  • Best long-range accuracy
  • Ideal for hunting

Power Adjustment Chart

Modification Power Change Difficulty
Increase pressure +10-30% Easy
Polish barrel +3-8% Easy
Heavier spring +5-15% Medium
Port enlargement +10-25% Expert
Lighter pellet +20-40%* Easy

*Velocity increase, not energy increase

Accuracy Tuning

Consistency Testing Method:
  • Fire 10-shot groups at 10 meters
  • Measure group size (distance between furthest holes)
  • Chronograph all shots, calculate average & std deviation
  • Goal: Under 1-inch groups, under 15 FPS variation
  • Adjust one variable at a time
Variables Affecting Accuracy:
  • Pellet fit in barrel (snug but not tight)
  • Pressure consistency (good regulator)
  • Barrel crown condition (critical)
  • Trigger pull (clean break, no creep)
  • Stock fit and stability

Advanced Modifications

Harmonic Barrel Tuning:
  • Add barrel weight/dampener
  • Reduces barrel whip during firing
  • Can tighten groups 20-30%
  • Experiment with weight position
Trigger Job:
  • Polish sear contact surfaces
  • Adjust trigger spring tension
  • Reduce pull weight (stay safe, 1-2 lbs minimum)
  • Eliminate creep and overtravel
Custom Cylinder Upgrade:
  • Larger volume = more shots per fill
  • Carbon fiber = lighter + higher pressure rating
  • Ensure proper threading compatibility
  • May need adapter bushing

Testing & Documentation

Keep Performance Log:
  • Before mods: Velocity, accuracy, shot count
  • After each change: Record improvements/issues
  • Best settings: Note optimal pressure/pellet combo
  • Problems: Document failures to avoid repeating

When to Stop Tuning

  • Reached desired performance level
  • Reliability starts decreasing
  • Parts showing excessive wear
  • Approaching safety limits
  • Accuracy gets worse despite changes

Remember: Reliability > Maximum Power

National Regulations (RA 10591)

  • Regulates firearms AND "deadly weapons"
  • Air guns may be considered regulated depending on power
  • Implemented by Philippine National Police (PNP)

  • Low-power air guns (under 7.5 Joules / 5.5 ft-lbs)
  • BB guns for recreational use
  • Toys and replicas clearly marked
  • Air soft guns (6mm plastic pellets, under limits)

MAY Require License/Permit:
  • High-power PCP air rifles (over 7.5 Joules)
  • Air guns capable of causing serious injury
  • Pellet guns used for hunting
  • Any air gun modified for increased power

  • Under 5.5 ft-lbs: Generally considered "toys"

üìñ Technical Glossary

Quick reference for pneumatic pellet gun terminology

A-D Terms

Accuracy: The ability to hit the intended target consistently. Measured by group size.
Airgun: Any gun that uses compressed gas instead of gunpowder to propel projectiles.
Barrel Crown: The edge at the muzzle end of the barrel. Critical for accuracy - must not be damaged.
Blow-by: Gas escaping past the pellet, reducing power and efficiency. Usually from poor pellet fit.
Break-in Period: First 100-200 shots where seals and parts settle into optimal condition.
Buna-N: Common O-ring material (nitrile rubber). Good general purpose seal material.
Caliber: Diameter of the barrel/pellet. Common sizes: .177 (4.5mm), .22 (5.5mm), .25 (6.35mm)
Chamber: Space behind the barrel where pellet sits before firing. Pressurized air expands here.
Chronograph: Device that measures projectile velocity in feet per second (FPS) or meters per second (MPS).
Consistency: How uniform shot-to-shot performance is. Key to accuracy. Measured by standard deviation.
Cylinder: Pressure vessel that stores compressed gas. Also called "bottle" or "tank."
Degassing: Process of releasing all pressure from the system before maintenance.
Diabolo: Waisted pellet shape - wider at head and skirt, narrow in middle. Most accurate design.
Durometer: Measurement of rubber hardness. Most O-rings are 70-90 durometer (Shore A scale).

E-L Terms

Energy (Muzzle): Kinetic energy of pellet leaving barrel. Measured in foot-pounds (ft-lbs) or Joules.
EPDM: O-ring material resistant to weather and ozone. Good for outdoor storage.
Fill Pressure: Maximum pressure cylinder is filled to. Typically 2000-3000 PSI for most guns.
Fill Probe/Nipple: Connection point where compressed air enters the cylinder.
Firing Valve: Mechanism that releases burst of air to propel pellet. Opens when trigger pulled.
FPE: Foot-Pounds of Energy. Standard measurement of muzzle energy (weight √ó velocity¬≤ √∑ 450240).
FPS: Feet Per Second. Standard velocity measurement in US/Philippines.
Grain: Weight measurement for pellets. 1 grain = 0.0648 grams. Typical range: 7-25 grains.
Group Size: Diameter of circle containing all shots in test. Smaller = more accurate.
Hammer: Internal part that strikes valve to open it. Spring-loaded.
Joule: SI unit of energy. 1 Joule = 0.7376 ft-lbs. Used in most countries for power rating.
Lands and Grooves: Raised (lands) and cut (grooves) spirals inside rifled barrel that spin pellet.
Leading: Lead deposits left in barrel from pellets. Reduces accuracy if excessive.
Leak: Unwanted escape of compressed gas. Can be external (audible hiss) or internal (weak power).

M-R Terms

MOA: Minute of Angle. 1 MOA = ~1 inch group at 100 yards. Accuracy measurement.
O-ring: Circular rubber seal that prevents gas leaks. Most critical wear item in PCP guns.
PCP: Pre-Charged Pneumatic. Air gun powered by pre-compressed air in onboard cylinder.
Pellet: Projectile fired from air gun. Usually lead, various shapes (diabolo, wadcutter, domed, etc.)
Porting: Size/design of air passages. Larger ports = more air flow = more power (to a point).
Power Curve: Graph showing velocity vs shots fired. Shows consistency and shot count.
Precision: How close shots are to each other (grouping). Different from accuracy (hitting target).
Pressure Drop: Amount of pressure lost per shot. Determines shot count per fill.
PSI: Pounds per Square Inch. Pressure measurement. 1 PSI = 0.069 bar.
Regulator: Device that maintains consistent output pressure despite falling cylinder pressure.
Reservoir: Another term for pressure cylinder/tank.
Rifling: Spiral grooves inside barrel that spin pellet for stability and accuracy.

S-Z Terms

Seal: General term for any O-ring, gasket, or component that prevents gas leaks.
Shot Count: Number of shots possible from one fill. Depends on cylinder size, pressure, and power setting.
Shot String: Sequence of consecutive shots. Used to evaluate consistency.
Skirt: Flared rear portion of diabolo pellet. Seals barrel and catches air blast.
Standard Deviation (SD): Statistical measure of velocity consistency. Lower = better. Under 15 FPS is good.
Teflon Tape: Thread seal tape (PTFE). Wraps on threads to prevent gas leaks at fittings.
Transfer Port: Hole connecting air reservoir to barrel chamber. Critical size for power.
Trigger Pull: Force required to fire gun. Measured in pounds. 2-4 lbs typical for target guns.
Tune: Adjust gun for optimal performance (velocity, efficiency, consistency, or accuracy).
Valve Dwell: Time valve stays open. Longer = more air = more power (but less efficient).
Valve Seat: Surface where valve closes. Must be smooth and undamaged for proper seal.
Velocity: Speed of pellet. Usually measured at muzzle. Higher isn't always better for accuracy.
Viton: Premium O-ring material (fluoroelastomer). Best for high temps and chemical resistance.
Working Pressure: Pressure range where gun operates (regulator output or direct cylinder pressure).

Common Abbreviations

Quick Reference:
  • BAR: Pressure unit (1 bar = 14.5 PSI)
  • CC: Cubic Centimeters (cylinder volume)
  • CF: Carbon Fiber (material)
  • CNC: Computer Numerical Control (precision machining)
  • DOA: Dead On Arrival (non-functional from factory)
  • ES: Extreme Spread (highest minus lowest velocity)
  • HFT: Hunter Field Target (competition type)
  • HPA: High Pressure Air
  • LDC: Lead Dust Collector (suppressor/moderator)
  • MPA: Megapascals (pressure unit)
  • MPS: Meters Per Second
  • NIB: New In Box
  • OEM: Original Equipment Manufacturer
  • SCBA: Self-Contained Breathing Apparatus (tank type)
  • TOTW: Tin Of The Week (pellet testing)
  • WTB/WTS: Want To Buy / Want To Sell
